Easter in Germany: Traditions and Holidays

Easter (Ostern) is a four-day celebration in Germany, featuring unique customs and two major public holidays.

The Easter Long Weekend

It starts with Karfreitag (Good Friday), a silent day, followed by Easter Sunday, and ends with Ostermontag (Easter Monday). Both Friday and Monday are public holidays nationwide.

Traditions: Eggs and Fires

German children look for hidden eggs delivered by the 'Osterhase' (Easter Bunny). In many northern villages, 'Osterfeuer' (Easter fires) are lit on Saturday night to chase away the last of winter.
