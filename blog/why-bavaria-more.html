Why Bavaria Has More Holidays Than Berlin

Ever wondered why your colleagues in Munich are off while you're working in Berlin? The answer lies in centuries of religious history.

The Religious Divide

Germany's holiday calendar is deeply rooted in the Protestant-Catholic split. Southern states like Bavaria (Bayern) are traditionally Catholic, meaning they observe religious feasts like Corpus Christi and Assumption Day. Berlin, with its secular and Protestant heritage, has fewer religious public holidays.

Regional Freedom

Under the German Basic Law, each state has the right to determine its own public holidays. This results in Bavaria having up to 13-14 holidays, while Berlin typically has around 10.

FAQ

Q: Does Augsburg have more holidays than Munich?
A: Yes! Augsburg has its own unique 'Peace Festival' on August 8th, making it the city with the most holidays in Germany.
