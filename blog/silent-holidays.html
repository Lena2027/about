What Are "Silent Holidays" (Stille Tage) in Germany?

In Germany, some holidays are officially designated as 'silent'. This means strict rules on noise, music, and even dancing.

The 'Tanzverbot' (Dancing Ban)

On silent days like Karfreitag (Good Friday) or Volkstrauertag, many states enforce a 'Tanzverbot'. Clubs must stop the music, and public events that are considered too loud or festive are prohibited. The idea is to respect a day of mourning or religious reflection.

Q: Can I play music at home?
A: Generally yes, but you must not disturb your neighbors. The ban mainly applies to public venues.
