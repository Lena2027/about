Shop Opening Hours During Public Holidays

One of the biggest shocks for visitors is the 'closed' sign on every supermarket door during a holiday. Here is how to survive.

The Ladenschlussgesetz

Germany's Shop Closing Law is strict. On public holidays, almost all shops must remain closed. This includes supermarkets, clothing stores, and malls. The goal is to provide a day of rest for workers.

Where to find exceptions?

If you're out of milk on a holiday, look at major train stations (Hauptbahnhof) or airports. Shops in these locations have special permits to open. Gas stations often have small convenience stores as well.

Stay Informed: Don't get caught out by unexpected closures. Consult the 2026 Holiday Calendar and plan your shopping in advance.

FAQ

Q: Are pharmacies open?
A: Most are closed, but there is always an 'Emergency Pharmacy' (Notapotheke) open in every area on a rotation.
